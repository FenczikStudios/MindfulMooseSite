    The key to watering your garden is to water deeply and infrequently. Instead of watering lightly every day, which encourages shallow root growth and makes plants more susceptible to drought, water deeply once or twice a week. This allows moisture to penetrate deeper into the soil, promoting stronger root development and healthier, more resilient plants. Additionally, watering in the morning is often recommended, as it allows plants to absorb moisture before the heat of the day evaporates it, reducing the risk of fungal diseases.
